About Tzabra Academy

Our Mission

Our mission is to deliver an exceptional education that seamlessly integrates cultural traditions and rigorous academic studies, fostering a vibrant sense of identity and a profound connection to heritage within the Greenwood Village - Lone Tree community.

Why Tzabra Academy?

With changing demographics and increasing cultural diversity in Colorado, there is a growing need for educational institutions that help students develop a strong sense of identity and connection to their cultural heritage. Studies show that children who maintain strong cultural connections tend to develop better self-esteem and academic outcomes. This highlights the urgent need for education that strengthens cultural identity, fosters historical awareness, and ensures that future generations remain deeply connected to their heritage.

Our Approach

Our school offers a dual curriculum, combining rigorous academic studies with cultural education. Students learn language, history, values, and traditions to strengthen their identity. To ensure affordability, we participate in a homeschool enrichment program, providing quality education with flexible, cost-effective options.

Homeschool Enrichment Program

Understanding Homeschool Enrichment

The Homeschool Enrichment Program (HSEP) provides supplemental educational funding for homeschooling families and private schools. This innovative approach allows us to offer high-quality Jewish education while reducing costs for families.

Lower Tuition

Families can enroll their children without paying full tuition, regardless of their financial situation. Participating private schools in the homeschool enrichment program are required to deduct 25% off the full tuition.

Higher Standard

The program ensures compliance with state-mandated secular education requirements (e.g., 90 hours/semester). Students receive structured instruction in STEM, humanities, and language arts, improving academic rigor.

Flexible Education

Parents can decide whether or not to participate in the program. This allows families to customize their child's learning experience by choosing specific subjects or classes.

Legal Considerations

Schools receiving state funding cannot discriminate based on religion in admissions or hiring. A minimum of 90 hours per semester of non-religious studies is required for state funds. HSEP-related funding is projected for July 2026.

Our Community

Community in Denver

The Greater Denver area has a diverse population with many families seeking quality educational options. There are significant numbers of children in the South Metro Area looking for educational alternatives. Currently, approximately 80% of students in the area are enrolled in public schools.

Current Education Landscape

There are currently several private schools in the Denver metro area, located 8-11 miles from the Lone Tree-Greenwood Village communities. Tzabra Academy aims to provide a closer option for families in these areas.

Community Events

Throughout the year, Tzabra Academy hosts events that bring together students, families, and the broader community. From seasonal celebrations to educational workshops, our events strengthen community bonds and provide opportunities for shared learning and celebration.
